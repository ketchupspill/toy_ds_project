2. Main Question

The broad question I aim to address: We would like to know which "kinds" of players are most likely to contribute a large amount of data so that we can target those players in our recruiting efforts.

My more specific question is: can experience and age predict how many hours a user will spend playing in the players dataset?

The data will be helpful because I believe there may be some correlation ie greater experience, in a certain age range corresponds more to a certain likeliness of a player playing more.

4. Methods and Plan

Method

We will use k-nearest neighbour model to predict a player's hours based on their experience and age.

Why is it appropriate?

It is appropriate because it can be constantly used for each new player as the training model can also become more refined and accurate in its predictions.**

Assumptions

Must assume each player actually is at the correct experience level. Must assume age was truthful.

Limitations

This may be too simple of a methodology and could result in unforseen problems if it turns out there isn't a strong correlation between the predictors and the desired result.

Model steps

  • I will choose a knn model because I think this is better than a linear model, as the predicates are not quantitative but categorical.
  • The data will be split 75 training 25 testing.
  • Only one split
  • I may use cross validation (n=5 for simplicity) if it proves to be more effective than without validation.
